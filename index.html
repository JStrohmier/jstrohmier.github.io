Jean Wears Many Hats

Hello, I'm Jean (Lee) Strohmier. I've worked in research and writing copy in response to grant proposals and government solicitations. Curating information that was available online, here are some accomplishments that I am particularly proud of:

  • Baked and decorated my best friend's wedding cake.
  • DIY-ed my own budget wedding (with help from family and friends, of course): Designed wedding invitations, dyed napkins, made centerpieces, made place cards, planned the family-catered menu...
  • Planned a pig roast anniversary party for 50+ people.
  • Located a teacup set from a coffee shop in Indiana for someone in Maryland.
  • Learned to play my ukulele.

Having access to information online is important for everyone. Up until fairly recently, organizations have approached web accessibility as a costly remediation effort when facing a discrimination lawsuit.

I want to help shift this focus from remediation to proactively being responsible and sustainable.

Master of information management with a background in psychology, I am especially interested in overcoming barriers to access and usability of Internet resources, the development of community spaces, and the advancement of literacy initiatives through technology.

This website is constantly under construction. I'm learning new things about web access and universal use every day.
