I Wear Many Hats

Hello, I'm Jean (Lee) Strohmier. With my Master of Information Management degree and a background in psychology, I am especially interested in:

  • Overcoming barriers to access and usability of Internet resources.
  • The development of community spaces.
  • The advancement of literacy initiatives through technology.

I've worked in research and writing copy in response to grant proposals, RFIs, an RFP, and other awards. Before that, I worked as a retail sales associate and ESOL tutor. I'm pretty good at:

  • Proposals = Reading the fine print and checking documents for issues.
  • Research = Digging into data and pulling out targeted information.
  • Sales = Interviewing clients about their preferences and striving for an experience that attracts and motivates clients to come back.
  • Tutor = Sharing skills, tracking progress, and motivating growth.

Curating information that was available online, I was able to:

  • Bake and decorate my best friend's wedding cake.
  • DIY my own budget wedding (with help from family and friends, of course): Designed wedding invitations, dyed napkins, made centerpieces, made place cards, planned the family-catered menu...
  • Plan a pig roast anniversary party for 50+ people.
  • Locate a teacup set from a coffee shop in Indiana for someone in Maryland.
  • Learn to play my ukulele.
  • Decorate a reading room for less than $50. Hellooo free stuff on Baltimore Craigslist.

Having access to information online is important for everyone. Up until fairly recently, organizations have approached web accessibility as a costly remediation effort when facing a discrimination lawsuit.

I want to help shift this focus from remediation to proactively being responsible and sustainable.

This website is under construction. I'm learning new things about web access and universal use every day.
